Kabir Das was a 15th-century Indian mystic poet and saint whose writings influenced the Bhakti movement.
Known for his simple yet profound couplets (dohas), Kabir emphasized the unity of God, rejecting religious orthodoxy and rituals.
He preached love, compassion, and equality, bridging the gap between Hinduism and Islam.
His teachings continue to inspire people across generations, promoting harmony and spiritual wisdom.
